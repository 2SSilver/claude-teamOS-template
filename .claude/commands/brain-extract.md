Extract reusable insights from the current project work into a raw note in `brain/raw/`.

## Steps

1. Review what was worked on in this session:
   - Read recent plan files, outputs, or context files in the current project
   - If `context/decisions.md` exists, read it: decisions are first-class extract material — especially ones made this session
2. Identify what is worth capturing:
   - Patterns or approaches that worked (or failed in instructive ways)
   - Decisions where the reasoning would be useful later
   - Problems that took significant effort to solve and could recur
   - Frameworks or mental models applied during the work
3. Write a raw note to `brain/raw/daily/YYYY-MM-DD-<project-slug>.md` using today's date
4. Raw note structure:
   - `# YYYY-MM-DD — <topic>`
   - `**Project context:**` — which project, what phase
   - `## What was done` — brief summary
   - `## Observations` — what was noticed (not polished)
   - `## Patterns noticed` — anything that felt like a pattern worth watching
   - `## Potential promote` — call out anything that might qualify for wiki (don't decide now, just flag it)
   - `## Open questions` — unresolved things that might matter later
5. Keep it rough. Raw notes are not polished — they are captures. Speed matters more than structure here.
6. After writing, report what was captured and flag anything that looks like a promote candidate.

Do not filter aggressively. Better to capture something marginal than to lose something useful.
