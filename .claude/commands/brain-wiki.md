Consult the wiki before starting a new approach, framework, or design decision in a project.

Use this at the start of work on a non-trivial problem — before designing an approach, proposing a structure, or choosing a tool. The wiki may already contain a relevant pattern.

## Arguments

Pass the problem or topic to look up: `/brain-wiki value stream mapping` or `/brain-wiki security review approach`

## Steps

1. Read `brain/wiki/README.md` to see what pages exist
2. Read any wiki pages that are relevant to the argument (topic or problem)
3. If no argument is provided, read all wiki pages (there should be few enough to scan quickly)
4. Report findings:
   - **Match found:** summarise the relevant wiki pattern and how it applies to the current problem. Note any gaps or adaptations needed.
   - **Partial match:** describe what the wiki covers, what it doesn't, and what would need to be figured out fresh
   - **No match:** confirm the wiki has nothing relevant and proceed with fresh design
5. If a relevant page was found, apply that pattern as the starting point — don't reinvent it
6. After completing the work, note if the approach extended, confirmed, or contradicted a wiki page — that's signal for updating or promoting

The wiki is a decision aid, not a constraint. If the wiki page is wrong for this situation, say so explicitly and explain why.
